A Field Glossary

Every world has its first words. The ones below belong to ours — some borrowed, some made, all of them doors. You don’t need any of them to begin; they will find you as the work does.

Words for the practice

Invitation — The oldest word we have, and the heart of the craft. A guide does not instruct; a guide offers an invitation — a doorway into noticing — and what you do with it is entirely, gloriously yours.

Sit spot — A place you return to, in every weather and season, until it stops being scenery and starts being an acquaintance. Every guide keeps one; most will tell you it keeps them.

The walk — Slower than you expect. A guided sequence of invitations, unhurried on purpose, in which very little happens and, often, everything does.

Council — How we speak and listen in circle: one voice at a time, from the heart, without fixing or advising. Harder than it sounds; softer than you fear.

The more-than-human world — A phrase from the philosopher David Abram for everything alive and present beyond the human — forest, river, raven, stone — named this way to remember that we are members of that world, not visitors.

Threshold — A crossing place; the moment before is different from the moment after. The Forest Threshold is our name for the doorway into all of this — the crossing where the forest’s invitations begin.

The five movements — Connect, Discover, Embody, Remember, Shine: the arc of a threshold crossing, from arrival in relationship to letting what was kindled be seen.

Wayfinding — The art of choosing your path when more than one calls. We take it seriously: if you are unsure where to begin, that is not a problem to solve alone — it is a conversation we would welcome.

Words for the paths

The paths — The five paths that lead from the source: guide certification, Nature as Medicine, the Academy of Place, the Alchemy of Pilgrimage, and Applied Partnerships. Paths, because each is walked at your own pace, and no two cross the land the same way.

Academy — We use the old sense: not an institution but a company of people practicing and learning together, over years, with care.

Practicum — The mentored season of practice — real walks, on real land, with real people — where training becomes craft.

Alchemy of Pilgrimage — The Opus Academy’s path of transformation through journeying: creative work, practice, and travel that change the traveler. Practitioners call it Alchimage — a word you’ll earn soon enough.

Atelier — The working studio of the Alchemy of Pilgrimage: where the journey’s materials become the journey’s art.

Company — What we call the group that travels a pilgrimage together: people who did not choose each other but found themselves on the same road — like a case of found treasures, composed by chance into something whole. Not meant to be permanent; meant to be valued while it lasts. Within the work, such a group calls itself an assemblage.

Words for the people

Guide — One who walks ahead only far enough to hold a door open. The forest does the rest.

Mentor — A practicing guide who accompanies your practicum season — someone who knows your name, your land, and your growing edge.

The admissions circle — The people who read every application. Not gatekeepers; hosts, composing each circle with intention.

Circle — How we gather, whatever the occasion. The shape is the teaching: no head of the table, because there is no table.

The land itself is the elder here and everywhere in our world. From our home in Prescott, Arizona, we work on land that has hosted many lives across its long existence — human and other-than-human, some now gone from the Earth — and many cultures, most recently, before those of us here now, the Yavapai and Apache peoples and their relations. We honor the land, and all whom it has held, and its infinite threads of relational connections, in which we and our work are embedded, which we live into with awe and love, and for which we are profoundly grateful.
