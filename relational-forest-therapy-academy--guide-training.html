Relational Forest Therapy Academy

Guide Training & Certification

A mentored, cohort-based pathway from first invitation to permanent certification.

The certification pathway

  • Six-month remote training. Live calls with trainers and your cohort, paired with extensive nature-connection practice outdoors. Completing it earns provisional certification as a Forest Therapy Guide.
  • Four-day in-person immersion. After reaching module four of the remote training you become eligible; you are guided by trainers and deepen the practice on the land. You have two years to complete this step.
  • Permanent certification. With both complete, you are a certified Forest Therapy Guide and part of the largest forest therapy guide community worldwide.

What the training includes

  • 14 modules with over 120 lessons and a downloadable manual
  • Live calls with trainers, plus small-group discussion calls
  • 30+ short videos recorded by different trainers
  • 40+ assignments and self-exploration activities
  • 20+ nature-connection activities taken outdoors
  • Guided practice — be guided remotely, then guide and debrief your own walks

Prerequisite for certification: a current Wilderness First Aid (WFA) or Wilderness First Responder (WFR) certificate from a third-party provider such as NOLS — typically a two-day course costing roughly $175–300 USD. The ability to respond to minor emergencies is an essential guiding skill.

Cohorts run throughout the year in a choice of two orders — start online first or start in-person first.

The land itself is the elder here and everywhere in our world.
