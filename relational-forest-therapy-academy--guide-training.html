Relational Forest Therapy Academy

Guide Training & Certification

A mentored, cohort-based pathway from first invitation to permanent certification.

The certification pathway

  • Six-month remote training. Live calls with trainers and your cohort, paired with extensive nature-connection practice outdoors. Completing it earns provisional certification as a Forest Therapy Guide.
  • Four-day in-person immersion. Once you are grounded in the fundamentals of the remote training you become eligible; you are guided by trainers and deepen the practice on the land. You have two years to complete this step.
  • Permanent certification. With both complete, you are a certified Forest Therapy Guide and part of the largest forest therapy guide community worldwide.

Certification is not a stack of content — it is a season of practice with a shape. You will learn the craft of the guided walk: the pace, the sequence, the sit spot, and above all the invitation. You will practice it on the land where you live, inside a cohort that knows your voice, with a mentor who knows your name.

The land itself is the elder here and everywhere in our world. From our home in Prescott, Arizona, we work on land that has hosted many lives across its long existence — human and other-than-human, some now gone from the Earth — and many cultures, most recently, before those of us here now, the Yavapai and Apache peoples and their relations. We honor the land, and all whom it has held, and its infinite threads of relational connections, in which we and our work are embedded, which we live into with awe and love, and for which we are profoundly grateful.
