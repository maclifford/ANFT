The land itself is the elder here and everywhere in our world. From our home in Prescott, Arizona, we work on land that has hosted many lives across its long existence — human and other-than-human, some now gone from the Earth — and many cultures, most recently, before those of us here now, the Yavapai and Apache peoples and their relations. We honor the land, and all whom it has held, and its infinite threads of relational connections, in which we and our work are embedded, which we live into with awe and love, and for which we are profoundly grateful.
